    About Us

    Barry’s Buns is family owned business operated by Joel Singer (a.k.a Barry) with the help and support of his wife, Jen. Joel utilizes his expertise in the kitchen and heads up recipe development and production of the tasty baked goods while Jen — a paralegal and real estate agent by trade — handles all marketing, brand development, event planning, and business administration.

    Joel was born a baker. He started his own cheesecake business when he was 14 years old and during high school, he was in a culinary competition and took first place winning a $40,000 scholarship from Careers Through Culinary Arts Program (C-CAP) to attend Johnson & Wales University in Rhode Island. Joel graduated with a degree in Pastry Arts and was most recently the Regional Bakery Associate Coordinator of Whole Foods Market for the Mid-Atlantic Region. His formal career in pastry began at the Pennsylvania Convention Center and prior to working at Whole Foods Market, Joel was the Assistant Pastry Chef at The Rittenhouse Hotel under Executive Chef Jean Marie LaCroix.

    It has always been a lifelong goal for Joel to have his own place and sticky buns/cinnamon rolls have always been a favorite of Joel since childhood. His fondest childhood memory is that of his dad regularly bringing home sticky buns for the family. Additionally, his dad, for many years before his recent passing, had his own business in a farmer style market. Joel loved the atmosphere and helping his dad take care of the customers, so when the opportunity arose for a location with the Market on the Fareway (also called the Chestnut Hill Farmer’s Market) in the Chestnut Hill neighborhood of Philadelphia, it was a sign that now is the time for Joel to open his own bakery.
